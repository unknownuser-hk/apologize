A Message of Apology & Remembrance

In memory of a loyal friend, and in fulfillment of his final wish.

Scroll

The Misunderstanding

Hi. I wanted to explain something important from last year because there was a misunderstanding that I never got the chance to clear.

A Shared Project

When you joined our class in 8th grade as a new admission, we didn’t really talk much. One day our English teacher said she would check everyone’s notebooks, and if it wasn’t complete there would be punishment. My notes were incomplete, so I asked you if you could send me the English notes. You kindly sent them to me on WhatsApp.

Around the same time, I was also working on a small personal project to create an app with my friend, Tenzin Dorzhiev. He was doing the actual coding on his computer, while I was testing AI-generated code and helping manage the files from my phone.

The Phone

At that time my phone had two WhatsApp accounts. One was my own WhatsApp Business with my number, and the other was normal WhatsApp connected as a companion device to my mom’s phone.

One day while we were working on the project, I gave my phone to Tenzin so he could send some project files from my phone to his PC. I stepped away for a short time while he was using it.

The Messages

While using my phone, he saw a number in my WhatsApp that didn’t have a saved name. When he opened the chat, he saw the notes you had sent earlier. He mistakenly thought that number belonged to a boy and started sending strange messages as a joke, pretending to talk like someone asking a girl to be friends.

He didn’t realize that the number actually belonged to you, my classmate.

Unfortunately, the messages were already sent and seen. You blocked my WhatsApp and also my mom’s WhatsApp before I returned and understood what had happened.

Realisation & Regret

When I came back and saw everything, it was already too late. I was shocked and very upset because I knew how wrong those messages must have looked from your side.

I scolded my friend a lot after that, but the damage was already done and I had no way to explain the truth because I was blocked.

When 9th class started, sometimes I would see you in class. I want to say something honestly — I was not looking at you because I was attracted or in love. I was only hoping that maybe one day I would get a chance to explain the situation and apologize properly, but that moment never came.

His Diagnosis

Some time after 9th class started, something very unfortunate happened. My friend Tenzin Dorzhiev was diagnosed with lung cancer. The doctors said his condition was very serious.

Even during that painful time, he remembered what had happened. He felt incredibly guilty for the messages he had sent from my phone without knowing the truth.

His Mother's Message

Today, on 6 March 2026, I received a message from his mother. She told me that before he passed away, he had asked her to send me a message.

She wrote to me that he had some problems because of his illness, and before leaving he wanted to say something important. She told me that his last words for me were:

"My friend, I’m really sorry for the work I did unknowingly. If you see her again, please apologize to her from my side. I seriously didn’t know she was a girl. As this is my last message for you… goodbye my best friend. We will meet again in another life."

She also told me that after sending that message she would block my contact, because he didn’t want me to feel emotional every time I saw his number.

A few hours after she sent that message, he passed away.

Conclusion

I know this message may come unexpectedly after such a long time, but I wanted to tell you the truth and fulfill his last wish.

I’m truly sorry for the misunderstanding and for any discomfort those messages may have caused you. I just wanted to explain what really happened and sincerely apologize.

Don't let my parents know, I told them that I accidentally renamed contact and sent some messages

Thinking you'll understand my situation. As an Introvert, I don't have much friends, and I lost one of my best friends

In loving memory of Tenzin Dorzhiev
